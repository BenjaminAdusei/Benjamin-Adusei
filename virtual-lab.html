🧪 Virtual Physics Lab

8 simulations | 9 AI features | Built for Ghanaian classrooms

🚀 Launch Live Demo

The Problem

In many senior high schools across Ghana, students learn physics from textbooks only. They rarely see a pendulum swing or a circuit light up. Concepts like electricity and motion remain abstract, and exam performance suffers. As a teacher, I saw this gap every day.

My Solution

A fully browser-based virtual lab that works on low-end devices and even runs offline after first load. No expensive equipment needed.

8 Simulations: Ohm's Law, Projectile motion, Wave simulator, Thermodynamics, Optics & Lensnes, Simple harmonic motion, series & parallel circuits, inclined plane.

9 AI Features:

  • AI-generated experiment recommendations based on student performance
  • Misconception detector – identifies and corrects common physics misunderstandings (e.g., "you think heavier objects fall faster? Let's test it")
  • Natural language Q&A – students can ask "What happens if I double the mass?" and get answers in English, Twi, Ga, Ewe, or Dagbani
  • Real-time simulation data display – velocity, current, temperature, etc., update instantly without external graphs
  • AI-generated personalized quizzes – topic-specific questions with instant scoring and feedback
  • Voice control & text-to-speech – speak commands or questions; AI reads answers aloud
  • Adaptive learning paths – dashboard recommends next topics based on quiz scores
  • Real-time feedback on simulation actions – AI explains what changed when sliders are adjusted
  • Summary report for teachers – detailed analytics on quiz scores, topic mastery, and misconceptions

Technologies Used

HTML5 Canvas JavaScript (Vanilla) WebGL (for 3D simulations) AI API (OpenAI / custom model) Service Workers (offline)

Impact & Results

Piloted with 30 students in a rural school without a lab. After two weeks of using the virtual lab, quiz scores on electricity concepts improved by 30%. Student engagement increased visibly – they asked more questions and explored simulations independently.

What I Learned

AI features are most helpful when they are contextual – a simple hint at the right moment is more powerful than a long explanation. Also, offline capability is non-negotiable in low-connectivity environments.
