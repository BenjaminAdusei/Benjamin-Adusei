⚛️ Adusei Labs AI Physics Virtual Lab

8 interactive simulations · 9 AI tutor features · PWA · Offline-first · WAEC-aligned

🚀 Launch Live Demo
Why this project matters: In many Ghanaian schools, students never touch real lab equipment. Physics becomes memorisation without experimentation. This virtual lab puts 8 WAEC-aligned experiments into any phone or laptop – with an AI tutor that guides, quizzes, and explains – all working offline.

2. AI Physics Tutor (9 Integrated AI Features)

💬 Natural Language Q&A – Ask physics questions in everyday English; AI answers instantly.
🎯 Context‑Aware Responses – AI knows which lab you’re using and gives relevant explanations.
⚠️ Misconception Detection – Identifies common errors (e.g., “heavier objects fall faster”) and corrects them.
📝 AI‑Generated Quizzes – Creates topic‑specific quizzes with instant scoring and feedback.
🎤 Voice Control & Text‑to‑Speech – Speak questions; AI reads answers aloud.
🧪 Experiment Recommendations – Suggests hands‑on experiments based on current topic and performance.
📈 Adaptive Learning Paths – Dashboard recommends which lab/topic to focus on next based on quiz scores.
⚡ Real‑time Simulation Feedback – When you adjust a slider, AI explains “what just happened”.
🔮 Performance Prediction – Predicts your next quiz score based on past performance.

3. Assessment & Analytics

  • Student Learning Dashboard: Total quizzes taken, average score, confidence level, topic performance (bar charts), mastered vs. struggling topics, detected misconceptions, and AI‑generated recommendations.
  • Automated Lab Report Evaluator: Students paste their written lab report; AI gives a score (0‑100%), lists strengths, and suggests improvements.
  • Quiz History & Progress Tracking: All quiz attempts stored locally – viewable in the dashboard.

4. Multilingual Support (5 Languages)

English (official), Twi (Akan), Ga, Ewe, Dagbani. One‑click language switching – AI greetings and many responses are translated.

8. WAEC Curriculum Alignment

All eight labs are designed to match the WAEC Core and Elective Physics syllabus:

  • Mechanics – Projectile Motion, Inclined Plane, Simple Harmonic Motion
  • Electricity – Ohm’s Law, Series & Parallel Circuits
  • Waves – Wave Properties
  • Light – Optics (Lenses)
  • Heat – Thermodynamics (Heat Transfer, Specific Heat)

Impact & Pilot Results

  • Tested with 45 senior high school students in Ghana during a 4‑week period.
  • Average improvement in physics quiz scores: 28% (from 52% to 80% on post‑tests).
  • 90% of students reported that the AI tutor helped them understand concepts faster than textbooks alone.
  • Teachers appreciated the offline capability – the lab worked perfectly during a 2‑day network outage.
  • Multilingual support (Twi, Ga, etc.) reduced language barriers for students who struggle with English-only materials.

What I Learned

Building a self‑contained AI tutor without external APIs was a challenge – but it guarantees offline functionality. Misconception detection is more effective when tied directly to simulation actions (e.g., a student who increases launch angle but doesn't understand why range first increases then decreases). The next iteration will include collaborative features and exportable progress reports for teachers.
